Why pricing isn't public yet

I'm a sole maintainer building this in public. Setting commercial pricing too early — before there are enough commercial conversations to know what's reasonable — leads to numbers that are either too high (and lose deals) or too low (and damage long-term sustainability).

So instead of putting up a number that I'd have to defend or change, I'd rather talk. Most early commercial users want a few specific things — perpetual licence vs. subscription, single-product vs. organisation-wide, distribution rights vs. internal use — and the right price depends on which combination matches your situation.

What's available right now

  • If you're building open-source software compatible with AGPL-3.0, you can use Kronroe today, free, with no further conversation needed.
  • If you're building commercial software and need different terms, email me. We'll work out a licence that makes sense for your situation. Early customers get founder pricing.
  • If you're not sure which applies to you, also email me. AGPL is genuinely viral in some software designs and benign in others, and a 5-minute conversation usually clarifies it.

What you can expect when pricing lands

When the commercial pricing is published, it'll likely follow this shape:

  • A per-product perpetual licence for shipping Kronroe embedded in a commercial application
  • An organisation-wide annual subscription for internal use across multiple products
  • OEM pricing for distributing Kronroe inside other developer-facing tools
  • Founder pricing for early customers who agree to multi-year terms before the public price ships

Existing licensees won't have their terms increased retroactively when public pricing arrives. The commercial licence text already exists; only the price is in flux.
